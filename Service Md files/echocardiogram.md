## 1. Our Diagnostic Ultrasound Center
Our outpatient diagnostic ultrasound center provides high‑quality heart imaging for patients and referring providers throughout the western Chicago suburbs.  
Every exam is performed using modern equipment that produces clear, detailed images to help your provider answer specific clinical questions.  
These ultrasound studies use sound waves only—there is no radiation exposure.

## 2. Available Exam Options
We offer the following exam options in this category so your provider can select the study that best matches your needs:

- **Echocardiogram**: Comprehensive cardiac ultrasound that evaluates heart structure, pumping strength, and valve function using two‑dimensional, Doppler, and color‑flow imaging.
- **Stroke and Vascular Screening Package* (Carotid + AAA Screening + PAD)**: Offered as a separate add‑on vascular screening package (carotid + AAA + PAD). It complements but does not replace the echocardiogram and is scheduled as its own visit.

## 3. What These Exams Show
During your heart ultrasound, we evaluate:

- Overall pumping function of the heart (ejection fraction).
- Valve narrowing or leakage and associated pressure changes.
- Wall‑motion abnormalities that may indicate prior heart attack or cardiomyopathy.
- Fluid around the heart (pericardial effusion) or structural abnormalities.

Your provider uses these findings alongside your history, physical exam, and lab results to make an accurate diagnosis.

## 4. Preparation Instructions
To help your heart ultrasound produce the clearest images, please follow these guidelines:

- No fasting is usually required; follow your cardiologist's instructions about medications.
- Avoid heavy caffeine or energy drinks for a few hours before your appointment if possible.
- Wear a two‑piece outfit so the technologist can easily access your chest.

If your appointment combines more than one ultrasound type, our scheduling team will review the best preparation plan with you.

## 5. What to Expect During Your Exam
Here is what a typical visit looks like at our diagnostic ultrasound center:

- You will lie on your left side while ECG stickers are placed to monitor your heartbeat.
- Warm gel is applied and the transducer is positioned at several spots on your chest to acquire different cardiac views.
- You may hear Doppler sounds as blood flow across valves is measured; the test typically takes 45 minutes to 1 hour.

Throughout the exam you are welcome to ask questions; our technologist will let you know when each part of the study is complete.

## 6. Results & Follow-up
- If a doctor order is provided, a detailed report is sent directly to your ordering provider so they can review results and plan next steps.  
- Your provider may recommend medication changes, additional testing, or follow‑up imaging based on the ultrasound findings.  
- Disclaimer: If there is no doctor's order, images will be provided after the exam on a CD. In order to receive a radiology report, a doctor's order is required.  

## 7. FAQ (Including Ultrasound Terminology)
- **Is this the same as an ultrasound?** Yes. The terms sonogram, sonography, and ultrasound all describe the same type of imaging test that uses sound waves.  
- **What is a sonogram?** A sonogram is the picture or moving image created during your ultrasound exam.  
- **What is sonography?** Sonography is the medical imaging specialty that uses ultrasound to evaluate organs, vessels, and soft tissues throughout the body.  
- **What is a sonograph?** Many people use sonograph to describe the ultrasound machine or the images it produces; both uses are common.  
- **Is an echocardiogram the same as a heart ultrasound?** Yes. An echocardiogram is the technical term for a diagnostic ultrasound study of the heart.
- **Does the stroke and vascular screening package replace an echo?** No. The package evaluates blood vessels in the neck, abdomen, and limbs, while an echocardiogram evaluates the heart itself; many patients benefit from both when clinically indicated.
