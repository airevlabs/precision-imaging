## 1. Our Diagnostic Ultrasound Center
Our outpatient diagnostic ultrasound center provides high‑quality pediatric imaging for patients and referring providers throughout the western Chicago suburbs.  
Every exam is performed using modern equipment that produces clear, detailed images to help your provider answer specific clinical questions.  
These ultrasound studies use sound waves only—there is no radiation exposure.

## 2. Available Exam Options
We offer the following exam options in this category so your provider can select the study that best matches your needs:

- **Abdomen – Complete (Pediatric)**: Child‑friendly evaluation of abdominal organs for pain, vomiting, abnormal labs, or suspected infection.
- **Abdomen – Limited (Liver, Gallbladder, and Pancreas)**: Focused upper‑abdominal ultrasound when the pediatrician is most concerned about liver or gallbladder disease.
- **Kidneys**: Renal ultrasound looking for urinary‑tract dilation, infection, scarring, cysts, or congenital abnormalities.
- **Kidneys and Bladder**: Combined evaluation of kidneys and bladder volume, often used in children with urinary‑tract infections or bedwetting.

## 3. What These Exams Show
During your pediatric ultrasound, we evaluate:

- Age‑appropriate imaging of abdominal organs to look for infection, obstruction, or congenital anomalies.
- Kidney size and drainage patterns in children with recurrent urinary‑tract infections.
- Evaluation of hips, head, or soft tissue when ordered by the pediatrician or specialist.

Your provider uses these findings alongside your history, physical exam, and lab results to make an accurate diagnosis.

## 4. Preparation Instructions
To help your pediatric ultrasound produce the clearest images, please follow these guidelines:

- Infants may usually feed as normal unless your child's doctor gives different instructions.
- Older children may be asked to avoid solid food for 4–6 hours before an abdominal exam.
- For kidney and bladder studies, encourage your child to drink water so the bladder is comfortably full.
- Have your child wear comfortable clothing and bring a favorite toy or blanket for comfort at our diagnostic ultrasound center.

If your appointment combines more than one ultrasound type, our scheduling team will review the best preparation plan with you.

## 5. What to Expect During Your Exam
Here is what a typical visit looks like at our diagnostic ultrasound center:

- Parents or guardians are welcome to stay in the room for the entire pediatric exam.
- The technologist uses warm gel and a small transducer, explaining each step in child‑friendly language.
- Most pediatric ultrasounds take 15–30 minutes, with short breaks as needed if your child becomes fussy.

Throughout the exam you are welcome to ask questions; our technologist will let you know when each part of the study is complete.

## 6. Results & Follow-up
- If a doctor order is provided, a detailed report is sent directly to your ordering provider so they can review results and plan next steps.  
- Your provider may recommend medication changes, additional testing, or follow‑up imaging based on the ultrasound findings.  
- Disclaimer: If there is no doctor's order, images will be provided after the exam on a CD. In order to receive a radiology report, a doctor's order is required.  

## 7. FAQ (Including Ultrasound Terminology)
- **Is this the same as an ultrasound?** Yes. The terms sonogram, sonography, and ultrasound all describe the same type of imaging test that uses sound waves.  
- **What is a sonogram?** A sonogram is the picture or moving image created during your ultrasound exam.  
- **What is sonography?** Sonography is the medical imaging specialty that uses ultrasound to evaluate organs, vessels, and soft tissues throughout the body.  
- **What is a sonograph?** Many people use sonograph to describe the ultrasound machine or the images it produces; both uses are common.  
- **Is ultrasound safe for babies?** Yes. Ultrasound uses sound waves, not radiation, and is considered safe for infants and children.
- **Can my child eat before the test?** For non‑abdominal exams your child may usually eat normally; our team will clarify preparation when scheduling.
