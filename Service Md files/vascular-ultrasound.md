## 1. Our Diagnostic Ultrasound Center
Our outpatient diagnostic ultrasound center provides high-quality vascular imaging for patients and referring providers throughout the western Chicago suburbs.  
Every exam is performed using modern equipment that produces clear, detailed images of your arteries and veins.  
These vascular ultrasound studies use sound waves only—there is no radiation exposure.

## 2. Available Exam Options
We offer several vascular ultrasound options so your provider can choose the study that best matches your needs:

- **Aorta Doppler**: Ultrasound and Doppler evaluation of the abdominal aorta to look for aneurysm, narrowing, or plaque.
- **Carotid Doppler**: Detailed study of the carotid arteries in the neck to measure plaque burden and blood-flow velocity.
- **Upper or Lower Extremity Venous Doppler**: Evaluation of the deep and superficial veins in the arms or legs to look for blood clots (DVT) or venous insufficiency.
- **Upper or Lower Extremity Arterial Doppler**: Assessment of arterial blood flow in the arms or legs to detect peripheral-artery disease and circulation problems.
- **Peripheral Artery Disease screening* (PAD)**: Noninvasive ultrasound and Doppler evaluation of leg or arm arteries to detect narrowing or blockages affecting circulation.
- **Stroke and Vascular Screening Package* (Carotid + AAA Screening + PAD)**: Bundled preventive screening that combines carotid artery ultrasound, abdominal aortic aneurysm screening, and peripheral artery-disease evaluation in a single visit.

## 3. What These Exams Show
During your vascular ultrasound, we evaluate:

- Blood-flow direction and speed in arteries and veins using Doppler ultrasound.
- Presence of plaque, narrowing (stenosis), or aneurysm in major vessels.
- Clots or reflux in leg veins that may explain swelling or pain.
- Areas of reduced blood flow in the legs or arms that may indicate peripheral artery disease (PAD).

Your provider uses these findings alongside your history, physical exam, and lab results to make an accurate diagnosis.

## 4. Preparation Instructions
To help your vascular ultrasound produce the clearest images, please follow these guidelines:

- For most vascular exams no fasting is required, though abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA) screening may require 4–6 hours without solid food.
- Wear loose sleeves or pants so we can easily access the arms or legs being evaluated.
- Avoid applying lotions to the areas being scanned on the day of your appointment.

If your appointment combines more than one vascular exam (for example carotid plus PAD screening), our scheduling team will review the best preparation plan with you.

## 5. What to Expect During Your Exam
Here is what a typical vascular visit looks like at our diagnostic ultrasound center:

- The technologist applies gel and uses a small transducer to follow the course of your arteries or veins.
- You may hear whooshing Doppler sounds as blood-flow patterns are measured.
- Depending on the number of regions being studied, vascular ultrasound can take 30–60 minutes.

Throughout the exam you are welcome to ask questions; our technologist will let you know when each part of the study is complete.

## 6. Results & Follow-up
- If a doctor order is provided, a detailed report is sent directly to your ordering provider so they can review results and plan next steps.  
- Your provider may recommend lifestyle changes, medications, supervised exercise, procedures, or follow-up vascular imaging based on the ultrasound findings.  
- Disclaimer: If there is no doctor's order, images will be provided after the exam on a CD. In order to receive a radiology report, a doctor's order is required.  

## 7. FAQ (Including Ultrasound Terminology)
- **Is this the same as an ultrasound?** Yes. The terms sonogram, sonography, and ultrasound all describe the same type of imaging test that uses sound waves.  
- **What is a sonogram?** A sonogram is the picture or moving image created during your ultrasound exam.  
- **What is sonography?** Sonography is the medical imaging specialty that uses ultrasound to evaluate organs, vessels, and soft tissues throughout the body.  
- **What is a sonograph?** Many people use sonograph to describe the ultrasound machine or the images it produces; both uses are common.  
- **Does vascular ultrasound replace angiography?** Ultrasound is a noninvasive screening and follow-up tool; invasive angiography or CT/MR angiography may still be needed for detailed pre-surgical planning.
- **Will I need the stroke and vascular screening package?** Your provider can help you decide whether combined screening is appropriate based on age, symptoms, and risk factors.
- **Can PAD screening prevent amputation?** Early detection of reduced blood flow allows earlier treatment, which can lower the risk of ulcers, wounds, and potential amputation.
- **Will the test require injections or contrast?** No. Vascular ultrasound and PAD screening use only sound waves, not dye or needles.
